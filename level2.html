Hum Through Your Nose

Focus: Ghunne (the 2-count nasal hum), including the cross-word tanwin merge idgham bi-ghunnah, which belongs to the ghunne family..
⚠️ Watch out: Kids rush the hum (under 2 counts) or sound a clear N on the tanwin instead of merging and humming it into the next word, as in hameedun-majeed and Muhammadin-wa. An-Nas also has a single ihfa, only flag it on the page here, do not drill it; ghunne stays the one rule you practice this level.

🤲 Rabbana Atina prayer

Rabbanaa aatinaa fi-d-dunyaa hasanatan wa fi-l-aakhirati hasanatan wa qinaa 'adhaaba-n-naar.
Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire. (Qur'an 2:201)

🤲 Allahumma Salli prayer

Allaahumma salli 'alaa Muhammadin wa 'alaa aali Muhammad, kamaa sallayta 'alaa Ibraaheema wa 'alaa aali Ibraaheem, innaka hameedun majeed.
O Allah, send blessings on Muhammad and his family, as You sent blessings on Ibrahim and his family. You are Praiseworthy, Most Glorious.

🤲 Allahumma Barik prayer

Allaahumma baarik 'alaa Muhammadin wa 'alaa aali Muhammad, kamaa baarakta 'alaa Ibraaheema wa 'alaa aali Ibraaheem, innaka hameedun majeed.
O Allah, bless Muhammad and his family, as You blessed Ibrahim and his family. You are Praiseworthy, Most Glorious.

Hide the silent n/tanwin into a soft nasal hum.

Stretch the long vowel, 2 to 6 counts.
🌑 Silent / sun-lam
Written but not pronounced, or glided.
