Give It a Bounce

Focus: Qalqala (the springy, vowel-less bounce on the letters qaf, ta, ba, jim, dal)..
⚠️ Watch out: Kids add a vowel to the bounced letter, saying a-HA-du or a-HA-da instead of a clean springy a-HAD. Bounce the stop letter with no vowel attached. Note the lighter cases: the ba in al-abtar (Kawthar) is a mid-word sughra (Kawthar has no strong stop-bounce, every ayah ends in ra), and yalid in Al-Ikhlas is a mid-word sughra, while ahad, samad, yulad and the final ahad are the four ayah-end strong bounces. An-Nasr has two sughra bounces: the dal of yadkhuluna and the ta of al-fathu.

Tajweed color key
👃 Ghunnah
Nasal hum about 2 counts.
🫥 Ikhfa
Hide the silent n/tanwin into a soft nasal hum.
🔗 Idgham (no hum)
The n vanishes and doubles into the next letter.
🔔 Qalqalah
Light bounce on silent q t b j d.
↔️ Madd
Stretch the long vowel, 2 to 6 counts.
🌑 Silent / sun-lam
Written but not pronounced, or glided.
